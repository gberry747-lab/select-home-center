What Does a Manufactured Home Really Cost?

An honest look at what goes into the price of a new home in Georgia, so you can shop smart and avoid surprises.

Buyer's Guide

It is the first question almost everyone asks, and it is a fair one: what does a new manufactured home actually cost? The honest answer is that the price depends on you, the home you choose, and where it is going. Instead of throwing out a number that would not really fit your situation, let us walk through the pieces that make up the total, so you know exactly what you are paying for and where the real value is.

The home itself is only part of the picture

When you see a home advertised, that sticker usually covers the house and not much else. The size and the builder drive most of that number. A cozy single-section home costs less than a large multi-section family home, simply because there is less house. The builder matters too. We carry Clayton, its budget-friendly TRU line, and Scotbilt, which gives us honest options at different price points without ever cutting corners on quality.

Options and upgrades move the number as well. Nicer flooring, a kitchen island, a fireplace, or a spa-style bathroom all add to the base price. None of that is required, and part of our job is helping you tell the difference between the upgrades you will love for years and the ones you can happily skip.

The costs that a low advertised price often hides

Here is where a lot of families get caught off guard. A price that looks great at first can climb quickly once the add-ons appear. When you are comparing dealers, ask what is actually included, because the real cost of getting into your home involves more than the house:

  • Delivery. Getting the home from the lot to your land.
  • Setup. Placing, leveling, and securing the home, then joining the sections on a multi-wide.
  • Trim-out and finishing. The steps, the skirting around the base, and the final touches that make it move-in ready.
  • Heating and cooling. An A/C and heat pump so the home is comfortable from day one.

At some dealers, each of those lands on your bill as a separate line. That is exactly how a "low" price stops being low.

What we include for free: every new home from us comes with delivery and setup within 100 miles, an A/C and heat pump, trim-out, steps, and metal skirting, plus a FREE Lifetime Home Warranty. Those are not surprise add-ons with us. They are simply part of buying your home.

If you do not already own land

Your home needs somewhere to go. If you already have a piece of land, wonderful, that part is handled. If you do not, buying land is its own cost to plan for, and prices vary a lot depending on the town and the lot. You will also want to budget for getting the site ready, which can include a driveway, a foundation pad, and running water, power, and septic or sewer. We are glad to talk through what a site typically needs so there are no surprises down the road.

Financing changes what "cost" even means

For most families, the real question is not the total sticker price. It is what the monthly payment feels like. Financing spreads the cost out over time, and the payment depends on your loan, your down payment, and your terms. We work with lending partners who specialize in manufactured homes and who help real families every day, including folks whose credit is not perfect. You can read more about how that works in our guide on how financing a manufactured home works, or start on our financing page.

Why we say "Call for Price"

You will notice our homes say "Call for Price" rather than a fixed number. That is on purpose, and it is in your favor. A real quote is built around the exact home you like, whether you have land, how you plan to finance, and what is included. We would rather give you an honest, complete number for your situation than an eye-catching price that grows once the add-ons show up. There is never any pressure, and the quote is always free.
