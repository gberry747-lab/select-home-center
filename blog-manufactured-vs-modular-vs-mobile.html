Manufactured, Modular, or Mobile? The Plain-English Difference

Three words that get mixed up all the time. Here is what each one really means, without the jargon, and which kind of home we sell here in Folkston.

Buyer's Guide

If you have started shopping for a new home, you have probably run into three terms tossed around like they mean the same thing: manufactured, modular, and mobile. They do not. Knowing the difference helps you shop with confidence and ask the right questions. Here is the plain-English version.

Mobile home

"Mobile home" is the older name for a factory-built home, and it is the one most folks still use out of habit. It really refers to homes built before June 15, 1976, back before there was a single national building standard for factory-built housing. After that date, the rules changed and the official name changed with it.

So when a neighbor says "mobile home" today, they almost always mean a manufactured home. It is a friendly, familiar term, and we are happy to use it too. Just know that the homes we sell are held to a modern federal standard the older ones never had.

Manufactured home

This is what Select Home Center sells. A manufactured home is built from the ground up inside a climate-controlled factory to a strict federal building code, often called the HUD code, then delivered to your property and set up on site. Every home is inspected as it is built, so quality stays consistent no matter the weather outside.

Today's manufactured homes are a long way from the older picture some people carry in their heads. They come with real drywall, name-brand appliances, energy-efficient windows, and floor plans that live like any site-built house. We hand-pick ours from two trusted builders:

  • Clayton, including its budget-friendly TRU line, one of the most recognized names in the country.
  • Scotbilt, a Southern builder known for solid, well-finished homes.

Because they are built on an efficient factory line, you get a lot of home for the money. And every new home from us includes delivery and setup within 100 miles, A/C and heat pump, trim-out, steps, metal skirting, and a FREE Lifetime Home Warranty that covers the kitchen appliances, plumbing and water heater, electrical, and the heating and cooling.

Are they good quality?

Yes. Building indoors means no rained-on lumber and no shortcuts hidden by bad weather. The federal code sets requirements for structure, fire safety, energy use, and more, and each home is checked against it before it ever leaves the factory. You can walk our lot, open the cabinets, and see the finish for yourself.

Modular home

A modular home is also built in a factory, but to a different set of rules. Instead of the federal HUD code, modular homes are built to the same local and state building codes as a house framed on site. They are made in large sections, called modules, then trucked to the lot and joined together on a permanent foundation.

Modular can be a fine choice for some buyers, but it is a different product with a different price and process. Select Home Center does not sell modular homes, so if that is what you are after, we will point you in the right direction rather than steer you wrong.

The short version: "Mobile home" is the old name, "manufactured home" is the modern one built to the federal HUD code (that is what we sell, from Clayton and Scotbilt), and a "modular home" is built to local codes and assembled on site (that is what we do not sell).

Which one is right for you?

For most working families in our area, a manufactured home hits the sweet spot: modern features, honest pricing, quick timeline, and that free lifetime warranty for real peace of mind. The best way to decide is to see one in person. Browse our current inventory, then come walk through a few and picture your family in them.
